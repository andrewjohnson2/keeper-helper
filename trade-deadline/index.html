2026 Trade Deadline

With Scotts Tots and Vladdy's Daycare battling at the top and The Old Timers a half-game back, the trade deadline is set to define the championship race.

June 28, 2026

With exactly 27 days until the July 25 deadline, the front offices of this league are about to make the choices that will define their franchises for the next three seasons. At the top of the standings, Scotts Tots and Vladdy's Daycare sit near the summit, with The Old Timers breathing down their necks just a half-game back. But standings in late June are a mirage; the true champion will be decided by who has the courage to address their glaring weaknesses right now, and who gets paralyzed by the opportunity cost of their future keeper slots.

In a league where keeper rounds dictate long-term value, this deadline is far more complex than a simple rental market. A player's trade value is a delicate equation: current production balanced against winter keeper cost and contract length. An elite starter like Chris Sale under contract through 2027 at a 4th-round cost is a franchise-altering bargain. Conversely, a rental like Shohei Ohtani who expires this fall requires no future keeper commitment but offers no security beyond October.

To map out the weeks ahead, we have synthesized all 12 rosters, draft pick allocations, and active contracts.

Scotts Tots: First Place, Zero Saves

Scotts Tots is the best offensive team in the league. Paul Skenes anchors a rotation that most teams would kill for. And yet, they have produced 16 Saves + Holds all season. Sixteen. That is not a gap—that is a chasm. The bullpen has been an active liability all year, and they are one playoff series away from watching a lead evaporate in the eighth inning because they have nobody to close it out. No contender with first-place ambitions should be going into October with this bullpen intact.

The fix is sitting right there on the pcorcoran3 roster: Jhoan Duran, 1.63 ERA, 20 SVH, lockable at a 4th-round cost. pcorcoran3 is rebuilding and willing to deal. For Scotts Tots, this is the most obvious acquisition in the league. There is no excuse for not having made this call already. If they want rotation depth on top of that, Cam Schlittler on Mount My Castle (1.62 ERA, 118 K) is available for the right price—though Mount My Castle's motivation to sell their cheapest ace is questionable at best.

Vladdy's Daycare: Co-First Place and Coasting

Vladdy's Daycare leads the league in runs scored (583) and has been the most complete offensive team all season. They have earned their spot at the top. They have also produced 24 Saves + Holds—barely better than Scotts Tots and still one of the weakest bullpen outputs in the league for a team with championship expectations. Their ERA sits at 3.29 and their WHIP at 1.179, which looks fine in aggregate, but those numbers are being carried almost entirely by the rotation. The relief corps is a tire fire waiting to happen in October.

Vladdy's Daycare is deep enough offensively to absorb trading a bat for bullpen help. Louis Varland on Mount My Castle (0.82 ERA, 21 SVH) is the best available reliever on a waiver tag—which means getting Mount My Castle to deal will require dramatically overpaying. Alternatively, Duran is right there if Scotts Tots doesn't move first. One of these two teams is going to fix their bullpen before July 25. The other is going to regret it by mid-October.

The Old Timers' Dual Problem

The Old Timers lead the league in home runs (178) and RBIs (535). They hit. Nobody disputes that. But they are bleeding ratios with a 4.09 ERA and 1.202 WHIP, and they have produced just 68 stolen bases as a team. Those two weaknesses—pitching ratios and stolen bases—are the difference between a first-round exit and a championship. Wheeler fixes the pitching. Bobby Witt Jr. fixes the speed. The Old Timers have the draft capital and the offensive surplus to go after both. The question is whether they are willing to pay the price.

Witt is on Sasaki Bombs with three years of contract control through 2028. He has 28 stolen bases by himself. Sasaki Bombs is selling. But they are not selling cheap—Witt is their best asset and they know it. The Old Timers will need to move Corey Seager and multiple top picks to get this done. If they pull it off, they become the most complete team in the league.

OnlyDans: The Backwards Team

OnlyDans has the best bullpen in the league. 63 Saves + Holds, comfortably ahead of everyone else. They also rank near the bottom in home runs (119) and OPS (.729). That is a team built completely backwards from where the league market is right now. The contenders desperately need saves. OnlyDans desperately needs bats.

The solution is obvious but the negotiation will be complicated. Yordan Alvarez on pcorcoran3 is the premier offensive rental—1.045 OPS, 25 home runs, expiring contract, zero keeper overhead. OnlyDans should be the most aggressive bidder. They can afford to move bullpen depth to fix their OPS problem, and they should. Miguel Vargas from the same pcorcoran3 roster adds 18 home runs, 10 stolen bases, and an .841 OPS at a 16th-round keeper cost. That is elite value.

Short Porch Soldiers: The Richest Seller in the League

Short Porch Soldiers sits in last place with a .338 win percentage and no realistic path back to contention this season. But their roster tells a different story in terms of trade value—they are holding the three best affordable starting pitchers on the market simultaneously. Chris Sale is posting a 2.14 ERA at age 37 and is locked in through 2027 at a 4th-round keeper cost. Chase Burns is arguably the best rookie pitcher in the league this season: 2.36 ERA, 112 strikeouts in 91.2 innings at age 23, signable in the 3rd round this winter. Dylan Cease leads the entire league with 128 strikeouts and is kept through 2027.

The catch is that Short Porch knows exactly what they have and will not give any of it away. They are looking for a prospect haul plus draft capital for each arm. Raisel Iglesias (1.37 ERA, 16 SVH) is another movable bullpen piece that has flown under the radar. Any contender who needs strikeouts, ratios, or saves depth should have Short Porch Soldiers at the top of their call list—but should budget accordingly.

We <3 PEDs: Full Teardown Mode

We <3 PEDs is the most motivated seller in the league. At .346 win percentage and 34.5 games back, they are not rebuilding around the margins—they are burning it down entirely. Zack Wheeler (2.03 ERA, 0.863 WHIP) and Jacob deGrom (3.55 ERA, 106 K in 88.2 IP) are both on the block. Wheeler is under contract through 2027. deGrom runs through 2028 at a 2nd-round cost, making him the most undervalued long-term asset at this deadline.

Their offense also has overlooked trade chips. Mickey Moniak is slashing .893 OPS with 12 HR in 163 AB off the bench and is a waiver pickup available to sign cheaply this winter. Gavin Williams (3.82 ERA, 111 K) and Robbie Ray (3.70 ERA, 80 K) add mid-rotation depth. We <3 PEDs has every incentive to deal now: contracts are expiring, the team is rebuilding, and waiting only gives buyers more leverage.

pcorcoran3: The Most Versatile Seller

pcorcoran3 is in 6th place but has clearly shifted into sell mode—and they happen to be holding the three most in-demand trade chips in the league across three different categories. Yordan Alvarez is the premier offensive rental (1.045 OPS, 25 HR), Jhoan Duran is the most coveted bullpen piece (1.63 ERA, 20 SVH), and Miguel Vargas is the best cheap multi-category bat available (.841 OPS, 18 HR, 10 SB, signable in the 16th round). Those three players address the biggest needs of every major contender, which is exactly why pcorcoran3 has more leverage than any other team at this deadline.

Beyond the headliners, Logan Webb (3.09 ERA, 10 QS) is an underrated depth starter under a no-cost keeper slot through 2027, and Oneil Cruz (.822 OPS, 14 HR, 21 SB) is a rental shortstop who changes the speed calculus for any team that lands him. pcorcoran3 should receive a king's ransom for their top two pieces and still have chips left to deal.

Sasaki Bombs: Selective Sellers

Sasaki Bombs is the most complicated seller to deal with because their motivation is uneven. Bobby Witt Jr. (.826 OPS, 28 SB, through 2028) and Yoshinobu Yamamoto (2.67 ERA, 0.891 WHIP, through 2027) are their two genuinely movable headline assets—both have real trade value and Sasaki Bombs has signaled willingness to listen. These are the conversations worth having.

Max Meyer (2.60 ERA, 107 K) and Foster Griffin (2.93 ERA, 98 K) are a different story entirely. Both are waiver pickups that Sasaki Bombs can retain next winter for essentially nothing, so their motivation to sell is minimal. Any buyer willing to dramatically overpay relative to stats might pry one loose. Jason Adam (2.37 ERA, 19 SVH) and Kevin Kelly (2.61 ERA, 21 SVH) are a legitimate bullpen duo that could also generate interest if Sasaki decides to move depth.

Mount My Castle: Rebuilding With Reluctance

Mount My Castle sits in 10th place and is nominally in rebuild mode, but their roster is so well-stocked with cheap, keepable assets that selling is harder than it sounds. Cam Schlittler (1.62 ERA, 0.920 WHIP, 118 K in 100 IP) is the most dominant pitcher in the league this season and can be signed next winter at just a 5th-round cost. Louis Varland (0.82 ERA, 21 SVH) is equally untouchable on a near-free waiver tag. Mount My Castle has no obligation to move either player, which means a buyer needs to bring an offer that compensates for what they'd be giving up long-term.

Shohei Ohtani (1.58 ERA, 0.904 WHIP, 86 K in 79.2 IP) is the more actionable deal: his contract is expiring this season, making him a pure rental with no keeper upside. Mount My Castle should be actively shopping him before the deadline. Matt Olson (.864 OPS, 20 HR) is also expiring and fits perfectly for a power-hungry contender. Eduardo Rodriguez (2.27 ERA in 95 IP) is a hidden gem in the rotation who could quietly attract late-deadline interest.

Kposer: Pitching or Bust

Kposer is one of the most confounding teams in the league. They are 5th in the standings but their pitching is an outright disaster—a league-worst 4.45 ERA and 1.300 WHIP that threatens to unravel everything their offense has built. With 162 home runs, 509 RBI, and a .767 OPS, their lineup ranks among the best in the league. The problem is that they are surrendering runs almost as fast as they can score them. At this pace, Kposer will get bounced in the first round of the playoffs by a team with better pitching ratios.

The fix exists. Cam Schlittler (1.62 ERA, 118 K) on Mount My Castle is the most dominant arm available, and Kposer has the draft capital and the offensive surplus to make a compelling offer. Max Meyer and Foster Griffin on Sasaki Bombs are more affordable rotation upgrades. Any one of these arms dramatically improves Kposer's ERA and WHIP. The window is open; but it requires moving draft picks they cannot afford to hoard if they want to be a real October threat.

The Veteran Bubble Dilemma: Fightin' Busch Lights

The most fascinating scenario at the deadline belongs to the Fightin' Busch Lights. Sitting in 7th place at .487 and one game outside the playoff bracket, the surface-level read is to sell. But pull up their roster and you will see why that is wrong: Salvador Perez (36), Nolan Arenado (35), Giancarlo Stanton (36), Jorge Soler (34), Pete Alonso (31), Willy Adames (30), Nathan Eovaldi (36), Carlos Rodon (33), Pete Crow-Armstrong (24), and Bryan Baker running a 1.95 ERA out of the bullpen.

This roster is built to win right now. The veterans are not declining pieces you shop for future assets—they are productive contributors in the final years of competitive windows. Logan Gilbert (3.42 ERA, 107 Ks) is under a 3-year keeper contract through 2028, making him one of the most valuable long-term pitching assets on the Busch Lights roster. Bryan Baker (1.95 ERA, 23 SVH) is a waiver pickup keepable at a 25th-round cost. Dealing either one to a contender is a mistake they cannot take back. The Busch Lights are one hot streak from the playoff picture. The correct deadline move is to add pitching depth, not strip it. This is their last window. Act like it.

gochargers: The Rebuild Blueprint

While teams like We <3 PEDs and Short Porch Soldiers are grabbing the headlines with high-profile aces, gochargers sits in 9th place (.450 win percentage, 22.0 GB) holding some of the most efficient keeper values in the entire league. They aren't just selling; they are positioned to execute the cleanest rebuild in the league if they play their cards right.

The prizes of the gochargers roster are outfielder Jordan Walker and reliever Robert Suarez. Walker is having a quiet breakout season (.859 OPS, 18 HR, 10 SB) and is signable next winter at a massive 21st-round discount. Suarez has been flat-out untouchable (0.56 ERA, 16 SVH, 0.844 WHIP) and carries a 17th-round keeper tag. Neither player does anything for gochargers in a lost season, but to a contender looking for cheap, high-impact depth, they are goldmines. Adding in secondary pieces like Erik Sabrowski (2.82 ERA, 19 SVH) or starter Taj Bradley could net gochargers a treasure trove of 2027 draft capital to fast-track their return to competitiveness.

With these team needs established, the deadline landscape is clear. Rebuilding sellers hold the exact pieces that these five contenders need to survive the summer. But talent is only half the equation; the true value of these chips is determined by keeper economics.
