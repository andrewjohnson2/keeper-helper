27 Days. Who Buys, Who Sells, and the Moves That Will Define This Season.

The trade deadline closes Saturday, July 25 at 8:59 PM PDT. Scotts Tots and Vladdy's Daycare are deadlocked at the top. The Old Timers are half a game back and loaded with assets. The window is closing for every team in this league.

By The Commish • June 28, 2026 • Deadline: July 25, 8:59 PM PDT
📰 State of Play 📋 The Market 🤝 Predicted Trades

We have an absolute dogfight at the summit. Following Scoring Period 13, Scotts Tots and Vladdy's Daycare are tied for first place with identical .612 win percentages, and The Old Timers sit just a half-game back in third. Three teams separated by half a game with the trade deadline arriving. In a league where a single blown save or a cold week from a corner outfielder can flip a matchup, standing pat is not a strategy. It is a surrender.

Championships are not won by assembling the most balanced paper roster. They are won by identifying your one or two category weaknesses and surgically eliminating them before the deadline. The teams at the top of this league all have glaring deficits. The question is whether their front offices are honest enough to address them before it is too late.

The league's keeper structure adds a layer of complexity that makes this deadline unlike a traditional rental market. A player's value is not just what they do between now and October—it is whether acquiring them costs you a keeper slot, and what that slot would otherwise be worth. A player keepable at the 4th round is a discount. A player keepable at the 1st round is expensive even if the stats are elite. And a player the seller can keep for free—a waiver pickup running at a 25th-round cost—is a player that seller has very little reason to move. That tension defines every negotiation at this deadline.

The Bullpen Crisis at the Top

The most embarrassing statistic in the league right now does not belong to a last-place team. It belongs to the first-place co-leaders. Scotts Tots has produced 16 Saves + Holds all season. Vladdy's Daycare has 24. These are the two best teams in the league, and their bullpens are historically bad. A single competent reliever completely reshapes the playoff odds for either team, which is why the closer market is the most important one at this deadline.

The best available reliever with a motivated seller is Jhoan Duran on pcorcoran3—1.63 ERA, 20 saves and holds, lockable at a 4th-round keeper cost this winter. pcorcoran3 is rebuilding and willing to deal. For Scotts Tots, this is the most obvious move in the league. Louis Varland on Mount My Castle is running a 0.82 ERA and 21 saves/holds, but Mount My Castle can keep him for essentially nothing as a waiver pickup. Getting them to the table will cost considerably more than his stats would suggest. That distinction between a motivated seller and a team holding a free asset is the entire bullpen market in one comparison.

The Rotation Market: Sale Is the Prize

The most valuable starting pitcher on the market is not the one with the best ERA. It is the one where the seller is motivated, the production is elite, and the keeper cost gives the buyer a genuine discount. That player is Chris Sale. A 2.14 ERA at age 37, under contract through 2027 at a 4th-round keeper cost. Short Porch Soldiers is selling. The math is clean. Sale is the #1 player on this board.

Cam Schlittler on Mount My Castle is the most dominant pitcher in the league this season—1.62 ERA, 118 strikeouts in 100 innings—and he is available to sign at a 5th-round cost this winter. Mount My Castle is rebuilding and willing to deal for draft capital. The problem for buyers is that Schlittler is so good and so cheap that Mount My Castle could also just keep him. They will need to be convinced with a compelling pick package. Scotts Tots, who desperately need rotation depth alongside Paul Skenes, should be leading that bidding.

Chase Burns has been spectacular for Short Porch Soldiers—2.36 ERA, 112 strikeouts in 91.2 innings at age 23. He is available to sign at a 3rd-round keeper cost. Dylan Cease is the strikeout leader in the entire league with 128 Ks, also from Short Porch Soldiers, locked in through 2027. Short Porch is selling hard, and any contender who needs strikeouts or ratio help should be calling. Zack Wheeler and Jacob deGrom are both available from We <3 PEDs, who are in a full teardown. Wheeler is elite on ratios. deGrom is the most underrated asset on this board: three years of contract control at a 2nd-round keeper cost is a deal that does not come around often.

The Old Timers' Dual Problem

The Old Timers lead the league in home runs (178) and RBIs (535). They hit. Nobody disputes that. But they are bleeding ratios with a 4.09 ERA and 1.202 WHIP, and they have produced just 68 stolen bases as a team. Those two weaknesses—pitching ratios and stolen bases—are the difference between a first-round exit and a championship. Wheeler fixes the pitching. Bobby Witt Jr. fixes the speed. The Old Timers have the draft capital and the offensive surplus to go after both. The question is whether they are willing to pay the price.

Witt is on Sasaki Bombs with three years of contract control through 2028. He has 28 stolen bases by himself. Sasaki Bombs is selling. But they are not selling cheap—Witt is their best asset and they know it. The Old Timers will need to move Corey Seager and multiple top picks to get this done. If they pull it off, they become the most complete team in the league.

OnlyDans: The Backwards Team

OnlyDans has the best bullpen in the league. 63 Saves + Holds, comfortably ahead of everyone else. They also rank near the bottom in home runs (119) and OPS (.729). That is a team built completely backwards from where the league market is right now. The contenders desperately need saves. OnlyDans desperately needs bats.

The solution is obvious but the negotiation will be complicated. Yordan Alvarez on pcorcoran3 is the premier offensive rental—1.045 OPS, 25 home runs, expiring contract, zero keeper overhead. OnlyDans should be the most aggressive bidder. They can afford to move bullpen depth to fix their OPS problem, and they should. Miguel Vargas from the same pcorcoran3 roster adds 18 home runs, 10 stolen bases, and an .841 OPS at a 16th-round keeper cost. That is elite value.

The Seller Landscape

Short Porch Soldiers is the richest seller on the market. They have three legitimate trade pieces—Sale, Burns, and Cease—and the motivation to move all of them. They will demand a haul for each. We <3 PEDs is fully dismantling with Wheeler and deGrom both available, and they have the most incentive to deal quickly before the deadline. pcorcoran3 is the most versatile seller: Duran, Alvarez, and Vargas are all available, covering bullpen, power hitting, and multi-category depth.

Sasaki Bombs is in a tricky position. They have four potentially movable arms—Witt, Yamamoto, Meyer, and Griffin—but Meyer and Griffin are waiver pickups they can keep for free. They have almost no incentive to deal those two unless a buyer overpays. Witt and Yamamoto are the legitimate trade chips. Mount My Castle is rebuilding but holds Schlittler and Ohtani: Schlittler is the bigger prize but harder to get since they can keep him cheaply; Ohtani is expiring and they should be actively shopping him.

The Veteran Bubble Dilemma: Fightin' Busch Lights

The most fascinating scenario at the deadline belongs to the Fightin' Busch Lights. Sitting in 7th place at .487 and one game outside the playoff bracket, the surface-level read is to sell. But pull up their roster and you will see why that is wrong: Salvador Perez (36), Nolan Arenado (35), Giancarlo Stanton (36), Jorge Soler (34), Pete Alonso (31), Willy Adames (30), Nathan Eovaldi (36), Carlos Rodon (33), Pete Crow-Armstrong (24), and Bryan Baker running a 1.95 ERA out of the bullpen.

This roster is built to win right now. The veterans are not declining pieces you shop for future assets—they are productive contributors in the final years of competitive windows. Logan Gilbert (3.42 ERA, 107 Ks, through 2028) is their best controllable pitcher, and Bryan Baker (1.95 ERA, 23 SVH) is their best reliever. Both are keepable for free as waiver pickups. Dealing either one to a contender is a mistake they cannot take back. The Busch Lights are one hot streak from the playoff picture. The correct deadline move is to add pitching depth, not strip it. This is their last window. Act like it.

The Market

Trade Deadline Player Rankings

Ranked by seller motivation, buyer opportunity cost, and current production. Not just stats.

1. Chris Sale

through 2027 · 4th round
SP · Short Porch Soldiers · 2.14 ERA · 1.048 WHIP · 99 K · 84.0 IP

The best trade chip in the league right now. Sale is pitching at an elite level—2.14 ERA, 99 strikeouts in 84 innings at age 37—and Short Porch Soldiers is clearly in full sell mode. The combination that makes him #1: the seller is motivated, the buyer gets two years of ace-level production, and the keeper cost is a 4th-round pick. For any buyer, a 4th-round slot costs almost nothing in opportunity cost. Nobody great gets kept at round 4. You are getting a $1 arm for a quarter.

2. Cam Schlittler

available to sign · 5th round
SP · Mount My Castle · 1.62 ERA · 0.920 WHIP · 118 K · 100.0 IP

Schlittler has been the most dominant starting pitcher in the league this season and nobody is talking about it. A 1.62 ERA and 118 strikeouts in 100 innings is not a hot streak—that is a statement. Mount My Castle is rebuilding, which means they would rather cash him in for 2027 draft capital than lock him into a 5th-round keeper slot while competing for nothing. For a buyer, the 5th-round opportunity cost is negligible. You almost certainly have nothing worth keeping at pick 5. Acquiring Schlittler and signing him long-term at a 5th-round cost is one of the best franchise moves available at this deadline.

3. Chase Burns

available to sign · 3rd round

Short Porch Soldiers is selling. The 3rd-round keeper cost is real—you are giving up a legitimate draft slot—but Burns is the kind of arm you build a rotation around for the next three years.
